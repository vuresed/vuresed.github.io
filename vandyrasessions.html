Supporting Student SurvivorsThrough an introduction to the neurobiology of trauma, Project Safe will share best practices for working with students impacted by sexual harassment and interpersonal violence. This session will explain what is going on in the body and the brain as persons experience, process, and heal from incidents of unwanted sexual contact and assault and offer proven strategies for assisting and supporting students in ways that avoid causing further harm.

MAPS Training
MAPS
This training introduces gatekeepers to the basic statistics, facts, and warning signs of suicide, as well as providing direct instruction on how to best facilitate referrals to trained professionals. From that grounding of basic knowledge and skills, this training will enhance gatekeepers’ ability to establish a more meaningful and positive relationship with individuals in crisis.
MAPS 2.0
Reviewing and building on the skills from the original MAPS, this 2.0 training allows gatekeepers to expand and deepen their understanding of modes of response. Returning participants will also have the opportunity to share prior challenges/successes for feedback and discussion.
Active Bystander TrainingThe Green Dot bystander intervention curriculum is a nationally-recognized, comprehensive approach to violence prevention that capitalizes on the power of peer and cultural influence across all levels of the socio-ecological model. Informed by social change theory, the model targets all community members as potential bystanders, and seeks to engage them, through awareness, education, and skills-practice, in proactive behaviors that establish intolerance of violence as the norm, as well as reactive interventions in high-risk situations – resulting in the statistically-proven reduction of violence when its methods are employed in campus communities. 
Housing AssignmentsA chance to review and hear about changes to the RCR, Roster Check, and THD processes.
Facing the Truth About Bias #ThisIsAmericaThis session purposefully focuses on everyday human interactions across similar and different identities, privileges, cultures, and experiences. Through intentional dialogue and engaging activities, RA’s will identify and address unconscious bias through the examination of one’s own identities in relation to systems of power and privilege. Through critical analysis, RA’s will deepen their understanding of unconscious bias and their individual role in working towards a more diverse, equitable, and culturally relevant residential community. 
Straight FactsThis training will provide participants with general information about the LGBTQIA+ community, including: language, cultural knowledge, and allyship. 
The Mindful BystanderBuilding upon the active bystander incident response skills cultivated in Green Dot training, this session offers participants the opportunity to learn more about why bystander intervention works and its effects multiply when modeled in campus communities. Mindfulness is scientifically proven to produce safer, calmer, more effective crisis response (and reporting of crisis response). Students will learn about the mindfulness-emotional intelligence-bystander intervention connection and leave with two mindfulness strategies (used by Google!) that can be quickly employed to ready active intervention in situations posing risk for sexual harassment and violence.

Being an AllyThis workshop will provide participants with tools for being active allys to all marginalized communities, and will include scenarios and “how best to respond” examples.
